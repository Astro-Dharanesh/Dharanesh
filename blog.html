Why This Blog Exists

Purpose & Scope

This blog is created as a personal academic journal to document my journey in physics and astronomy. It serves as a space where I consolidate my learning, research experiences, and observations while working with real astronomical data and scientific literature.

My primary motivation for creating this blog is to maintain a transparent and structured record of my academic growth. Rather than treating learning as a collection of isolated activities, this platform allows me to connect coursework, workshops, research projects, and outreach experiences into a coherent narrative.

The articles published here fall broadly into three categories:

  • Research Notes: Technical write-ups based on data analysis, observational studies, and ongoing research projects.
  • Conceptual Articles: Explanations of astronomical and physical concepts aimed at students and enthusiasts.
  • Outreach & Reflection: Summaries of seminars, workshops, conferences, and public science activities.

In the long term, this blog also functions as a research portfolio. It reflects my academic interests in planetary science, exoplanet studies, heliophysics, and optical–infrared astronomy, and demonstrates my ability to communicate scientific ideas clearly and responsibly.

All posts are written with an emphasis on scientific accuracy, clarity, and reproducibility wherever possible. When appropriate, references, datasets, and tools used in the analysis are explicitly mentioned.

Readers are encouraged to explore the articles listed alongside this page, which represent both completed studies and evolving research directions.
