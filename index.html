About Me

I am Dharanesh, a Physics student passionate about astrophysics, space science, and data analysis. My interests include exoplanets heliophysics, telescope observations, and planetary science. I enjoy working with real astronomical data and exploring ways to make space more understandable through outreach and research.

Projects

  • Exoplanet Light Curve Analysis: Analyzed stellar light curves using Python and Lightkurve to detect potential transiting exoplanets.
  • Asteroid Tracking (IASC): Processed FITS images from Pan-STARRS to identify asteroid 2021 CP66 and calculate its motion parameters.
  • Solar Feature Classification: Classified Active Regions, Coronal Holes, and Quiet Sun zones using intensity thresholds from AIA images.
  • Public Outreach: Organized telescope nights and astronomy programs as part of local science initiatives and as my department's Astro club secretary.
